# ORCA Market Opportunity Council

## What This Skill Is

A specialized council for deciding whether a business, wedge, niche, or market opportunity is actually worth pursuing.

Use this when the question is market attractiveness and buyer reality, not just idea creativity.

## Trigger

Use when the user wants:

- a marketable business idea
- a candid read on whether buyers will pay
- help choosing between opportunities
- pressure testing on niche versus venture-scale fit

## Five Council Roles

1. Buyer Economics Reviewer
2. Pain And Urgency Reviewer
3. Competitive And Distribution Reviewer
4. Founder-Fit Reviewer
5. Contrarian Kill-Switch Reviewer

## Stage-1 Expectations

Each worker should judge:

- who pays
- why they would pay now
- what has to be displaced
- what distribution path might work
- why the opportunity may fail even if the product is good

## Stage-3 Synthesis Must Produce

- pursue, park, kill, or revise-and-retest
- best plausible buyer and wedge
- strongest disqualifying concern
- market type:
  - venture-scale candidate
  - strong niche or lifestyle business
  - unclear but promising wedge
  - weak or overcrowded market
- next research or validation move

## Required Outputs

At minimum, contribute to:

- `templates/idea-scorecard.md`
- `templates/idea-decision.md`
- `templates/opportunity-memo.md`
- `templates/validation-plan.md`

## Failure Modes

- confusing broad TAM with reachable demand
- mistaking founder enthusiasm for customer urgency
- ignoring distribution difficulty
- recommending pursuit without a real next validation step

## Relationship To Other ORCA Framework Skills And Commands

Pairs with `orca-business-ideation`, `orca-research`, `orca-evaluate-idea`, and `orca-validate-idea`.
