# Idea Evaluation Lenses

ORCA Framework evaluates ideas through written lenses instead of a single fake universal score.

## Primary Lenses

### Founder Or Team Fit

Ask:

- why this team
- what unfair insight, access, or trust position exists
- whether the team appears likely to understand the problem deeply
- whether the team is likely to keep learning if the first solution is wrong

### Problem Quality

Ask:

- whether the problem is real
- whether it is painful, frequent, expensive, risky, or politically charged
- whether users already spend time, money, or process effort dealing with it
- whether workarounds already exist

### Market Quality

Ask:

- whether the market is already real or becoming real
- whether there is a reachable initial wedge
- whether the wedge is narrow enough to win but broad enough to matter
- whether timing is favorable

### Competitive Landscape

Ask:

- who the direct and indirect alternatives are
- how customers solve the problem today
- why incumbents or prior startups have not solved it well enough
- what creates room to win

### Mechanism And Execution Logic

Ask:

- how the idea changes outcomes in practice
- what the critical workflow or system change is
- what must be true operationally for the idea to work
- where bottlenecks or dependencies appear

### Defensibility And Asymmetry

Ask:

- what is difficult to copy
- whether learning loops, distribution, trust, data, workflow embedment, or network effects might matter
- whether this team can build an asymmetric advantage

### Evidence Versus Assumption

For each major claim, classify it as:

- observed
- inferred
- assumed

Then ask which assumed claims actually drive the decision.

## Scoring Guidance

Use [templates/idea-scorecard.md](../templates/idea-scorecard.md) only as a summary aid. A written argument is the primary output. A mediocre score with strong evidence may be better than a high score built on unsupported claims.
