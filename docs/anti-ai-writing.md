# Anti-Generic Writing

AI-generated writing often fails because it is smooth without being specific. It uses balanced paragraphs, generic transitions, abstract claims, and safe phrasing that sounds competent but unowned.

## Signals To Watch

- Repetitive sentence cadence
- Predictable paragraph rhythm
- Abstract claims with low specificity
- Generic transitions and filler phrases
- Over-explaining obvious points
- Marketing fog
- Polished but impersonal voice
- Missing concrete examples, constraints, or point of view
- No sense of what the writer would cut, defend, or prioritize

## Calibration Questions

Before drafting, answer:

- Who is the real audience?
- What do they already know?
- What would make them trust this?
- What specific examples or constraints belong here?
- What vocabulary fits the domain?
- What claims should be avoided because they are too broad?
- What sentence rhythm fits the medium?

## Grounded Voice

Authenticity does not mean fake personal anecdotes. Use concrete details, defensible claims, natural rhythm, and visible judgment. Avoid inventing lived experience. Prefer specificity over artificial intimacy.

## Review

Writing review should check rhythm, specificity, tone, filler, and whether the piece has a real point of view. If it sounds like a generic marketing page or interchangeable documentation, revise before shipping.
