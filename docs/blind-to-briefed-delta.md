# Blind-To-Briefed Delta

The blind-to-briefed delta compares what a first-look tester experienced with what a lightly briefed tester experienced later.

## Purpose

The delta report identifies product clarity issues that disappear once context is provided. Those gaps often reveal onboarding, copy, information architecture, or visual hierarchy problems.

## What It Compares

- Product inference
- Confusion points
- Task completion outcomes
- Trust and clarity signals
- Blockers found blind
- Blockers remaining after briefing
- Issues that briefing hides but users would still encounter

## Product Insight

If a problem disappears after briefing, the product may not be self-explanatory enough. If it remains after briefing, the issue is likely deeper: functionality, accessibility, performance, or interaction quality.

## Linear Integration

Post the delta report to the same issue as the blind and guided QA reports. Use it before ship readiness when first impression matters.

## Ship Impact

A clean guided QA pass does not erase a poor blind pass. Ship readiness should consider whether first-look confusion is acceptable for the release.
