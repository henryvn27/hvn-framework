# Example Opportunity Memo

## Opportunity Summary

Small event operators appear to have a real vendor-intake coordination problem that is frequent, operationally ugly, and poorly served by generic tools.

## Who Has The Problem

Operators of recurring events with enough vendors to create process overhead, but not enough staff to justify custom software.

## Why The Problem Matters

The cost shows up as deadline misses, manual follow-up, vendor frustration, and event-week chaos.

## Why This Approach May Work

The team can start with one narrow workflow where the value is easy to explain and early feedback can be gathered without a large build.

## Strongest Positive Signals

- clear current workaround behavior
- founder access to likely pilot users
- operational pain rather than vague preference

## Major Risks And Weak Spots

- market may be narrower than it first appears
- willingness to pay is not yet demonstrated
- generic tools may be good enough for many operators

## Evidence Versus Assumption Summary

The workflow pain is plausible and grounded. The monetization and long-term expansion story remain assumptions.

## Opportunity Type

Unclear but promising niche.

## Recommended Next Step

Run focused customer interviews, then test a concierge or manual workflow offer before investing in a larger product build.
