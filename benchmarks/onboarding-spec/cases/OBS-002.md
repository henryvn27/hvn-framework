# Benchmark Case OBS-002

## Case ID

OBS-002

## Scenario

A team requests an "AI support inbox" without specifying privacy limits, ownership, escalation rules, or supported channels.

## Input Prompt

"We need an AI inbox for support. Make a spec so we can build it fast."

## Expected Clarification Areas

- supported channels
- data sensitivity and privacy constraints
- escalation path to humans
- performance or SLA expectations
- excluded channels or unsupported automations

## Expected Scope Separation

- triage vs auto-response
- admin tooling vs customer-facing flows
- MVP workflow vs future analytics or training systems

## Expected Spec Quality Signals

- flags privacy and escalation as first-class constraints
- separates MVP scope from future automation
- includes verification-ready acceptance criteria

## Failure Patterns To Watch For

- assumes full auto-response is in scope
- ignores privacy and escalation risk
- creates a spec with no operational boundaries

## Scoring Notes

Strong runs will slow down enough to define boundaries and risk before writing a build-ready spec.
